About This Site

Ramen Guide Japan is a simple, English-language guide to help tourists discover great ramen restaurants in Japan — starting with Tokyo and Osaka.

Why We Built This

Japan has thousands of incredible ramen restaurants, but most menus and review sites are in Japanese. We wanted to create a friendly, easy-to-use resource for international visitors who want to experience authentic ramen without the language barrier.

How to Use This Site

  • Browse by city — Tokyo or Osaka.
  • Read the English description and check the price range.
  • Tap "Open in Google Maps" to navigate directly to the restaurant.
  • Use the tags (e.g. late-night, vegetarian-option) to find the right place for you.

Ramen Types Explained

Tonkotsu
Rich, creamy pork bone broth. Originally from Fukuoka. Very popular across Japan.
Shoyu
Soy sauce-based broth. Usually a clearer, brown soup with a savoury depth.
Miso
Fermented soybean paste broth. Hearty and complex — often paired with corn and butter in Hokkaido style.
Shio
Salt-based broth. The lightest and most delicate style — lets the broth ingredients shine.
Tsukemen
Dipping ramen — cold or room-temperature noodles served separately from a hot, concentrated broth.
